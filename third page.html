HIKKADUWA

Turtles In Hikkaduwa

The Hatchery

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is a conservation center committed to protecting sea turtles. It rescues and rehabilitates injured turtles, raises awareness about conservation, and releases hatchlings into the ocean. Visitors can learn about turtle species, witness hatchings, and participate in the release process, supporting marine preservation and eco-tourism.

The Goal

The main goal of Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is to contribute to the conservation and preservation of sea turtles. They aim to achieve this by rescuing and rehabilitating injured and orphaned turtles, protecting turtle nesting sites, promoting public awareness about the importance of turtle conservation, and releasing hatchlings into the ocean. Ultimately, their efforts are aimed at increasing the population of sea turtles and ensuring their long-term survival in the wild.

Why Hikkaduwa?

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Center in Hikkaduwa is located in this popular tourist destination on Sri Lanka's southwestern coast due to its suitable natural habitat for sea turtles, conservation efforts to protect them, the opportunity to raise awareness among tourists, and its accessibility from major cities.
