ESTABLISHED BY

In 1978, Dr. Upen de Silva and Dr. R. S. B Wickremsinghe founded the first turtle hatchery in Sri Lanka.

OUR LONG-TERM COMMITMENT

Our aim is to promote environmental conservation and protect Sri Lanka's wildlife and habitats, particularly endangered species. Our primary goal is to preserve nature for future generations by leading, facilitating, and inspiring people to take actions to safeguard the environment, including national habitats, plants, and animals. We strive to motivate individuals to contribute to the conservation of flora, fauna, biodiversity, and the natural environment in any way they can.

HOW IT STARTED

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery began as Sri Lanka's pioneering conservation and research project in early 1978. During that time, all seven species of sea turtles were classified as Endangered. Five of these species were discovered to regularly lay their eggs on the southern coast of Sri Lanka's beaches. Local villagers used to collect these eggs and sell them in the market.

OUR MISSION

We are dedicated to the conservation of sea turtles and the dissemination of knowledge worldwide.

OUR VISION

Our vision is to ensure the survival of sea turtles for future generations.
