Why Are Sea Turtles Important?

CORAL

Hawksbill turtles play a crucial role in coral reefs as they feed on sea sponges, preventing overgrowth and promoting biodiversity. Without them, sponges can smother corals, leading to their decline. The presence of hawksbill turtles becomes vital for maintaining the health and balance of coral reef ecosystems.

BEACHES

Nesting for sea turtles also has positive effects out of water. Discarded eggshells and unhatched eggs nourish dune vegetation such as beachgrass and provide important nutrients that stabilize dunes and prevent coastal erosion. 

ECOTOURISM

Sea turtles are also an important source of income for coastal residents through turtle-watching ecotourism. Studies show that ecotourism for sea turtles can generate three times more revenue than selling turtle parts (eggs, meat and shells), making them worth more alive than dead.
