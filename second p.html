Turtle Categories

Green Turtle

The green turtle is an endangered sea turtle known for its green-colored fat, herbivorous diet, and nesting habits. They inhabit tropical waters, graze on seagrass, and migrate to nesting beaches. Conservation efforts aim to protect their habitats and raise awareness about their ecological importance to marine ecosystems.

Olive Ridley Turtle

The olive ridley sea turtle is a vulnerable species known for its olive-green shell. They nest in mass groups called "arribadas" on specific beaches. Found in warm oceans, they feed on various marine organisms. Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting sites and reducing threats such as bycatch and habitat loss.

Hawksbill Turtle

The hawksbill turtle is a critically endangered species known for its distinct appearance and specialized diet. Found in tropical waters, they inhabit coral reefs and rocky coastlines. Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting sites and addressing threats such as habitat loss, pollution, and illegal trade of their shells.

Leather back Turtle

The leatherback turtle is the largest sea turtle species, known for its leathery shell and carnivorous diet. They have a global distribution and undertake long-distance migrations. Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting sites, addressing threats like habitat degradation and fishing bycatch, and raising awareness about their conservation needs.

Loggerhead Turtle

The loggerhead turtle is a species of sea turtle known for its large head and strong jaws. They inhabit oceans worldwide, feed on a variety of prey, and nest on beaches. Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting sites, addressing threats like habitat loss and fishing bycatch, and raising awareness about their vulnerability.
